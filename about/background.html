Theoretical BackgroundÔÉÅ

Density Functional Theory (DFT) is one of the most popular electronic structure calculation methods due to its combination of reasonable accuracy and computational cost. MALA works within the Born-Oppenheimer approximation (i.e. fixed ionic positions \(\underline{\boldsymbol{R}}\)) and takes the electronic temperature \(\tau\) into account.

In DFT, the central quantity is the electronic density of a given system. Within the Kohn-Sham framework, this density is given by

\[n(\boldsymbol{r}) = \sum_j f^\tau(\epsilon_j^\tau)\, |\phi_j(\boldsymbol{r})|^2 \; .\]

Here, \(\phi_j(\boldsymbol{r})\) denote the Kohn-Sham wave functions, which are given by the Kohn-Sham equations

\[\left[-\frac{1}{2}\nabla^2 + v_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}}^\tau(\boldsymbol{r})\right] \phi_j(\boldsymbol{r}) = \epsilon_j^\tau \phi_j(\boldsymbol{r}) \; ,\]

which give a system of non-interacting particles restricted to reproduce the density of the interacting system. The total free energy is evaluated using

\[A_\mathrm{total} = T_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}} [\phi_j] - k_\mathrm{B}\tau S_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}} [\epsilon_j] + E_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle H}} [n] + E_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle XC}}[n] + \int d\boldsymbol{r}\, n(\boldsymbol{r}) v(\boldsymbol{r})\; ,\]

with the external potential created by the ions \(v(\boldsymbol{r})\), kinetic energy of the non-interacting system \(T_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}}\), the electronic entropy of the non-interacting system \(S_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}}\), the electrostatic interaction energy of the density with itself \(E_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle H}}\) and the exchange-correlation energy \(E_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle XC}}\).

Forces and other quantities of interest can be derived from the total energy.

MALA aims to approximate this total energy by using Neural Networks (NN) \(M\) to learn the Local Density of States (LDOS), which is defined as

\[d(\epsilon, \boldsymbol{r}) = \sum_j |\phi_j(\boldsymbol{r})|^2 \delta(\epsilon-\epsilon_j^\tau) \; ,\]

and connected to the electronic density via

\[n(\boldsymbol{r}) = \int d \epsilon\; f^\tau(\epsilon) d(\epsilon, \boldsymbol{r}) \; ,\]

and the electronic density of states via

\[D(\epsilon) = \int d\boldsymbol{r} \; d(\epsilon, \boldsymbol{r}) \; .\]

These connections are important, since the \(T_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}}\) and \(S_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}}\) can be expressed in terms of \(D\). MALA therefore evaluates

\[A_\mathrm{total} = E_b\big[D[d]\big] - k_\mathrm{B}\tau S_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle S}} \big[D[d]\big] - E_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle H}} \big[n[d]\big] + E_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle XC}}\big[n[d]\big] + \int d\boldsymbol{r}\, n[d](\boldsymbol{r}) v_\mathrm{{\scriptscriptstyle XC}}(\boldsymbol{r})\; ,\]

with the band energy \(E_b\).

If the LDOS can be learned appropriately accurate, MALA can therefore, evaluate energies analytically. To approximate the LDOS, an NN, \(M\), is employed via

\[d(\epsilon, \boldsymbol{r}) = M(B(j, \boldsymbol{r})) [\boldsymbol{\lambda}] \; .\]

Thus, a network pass is performed for every point in space. \(B(j, \boldsymbol{r})\) are some descriptors that capture the local environment. Often SNAP descriptors are used. \(\boldsymbol{\lambda}\) are the hyperparameters that characterize the neural network.
